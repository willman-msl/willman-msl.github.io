    Materials Simulation
    Laboratory

    Uncovering the behavior of materials at atomic-scale
    by theory, simulations and experiment


    MSL research program focuses on predicting properties of materials at the atomic scale by uniquely coupling quantum and classical molecular dynamics (MD) simulations with experiment. We apply machine learning and leadership class high performance computing (HPC) to achieve quantum accurate description of interatomic interactions in billion atom simulations at experimental time and length scales. Our main science goal is to uncover the behavior of matter subjected to extreme pressures (many millions of atmospheres (Mbars)) and temperatures (tens of thousands of kelvins) in the interiors of exoplanets, during inertial confinement fusion implosions and shock compression of condensed matter. We strive to achieve transformative advances by directly translating the unprecedented power of extreme-scale quantum accurate MD simulations on leadership class HPC systems into important science discoveries through close interaction and interplay between theory, simulations and experiment.


    Matter at Extreme Conditions

    We study inelastic deformations, phase transitions, and chemical reactions in statically and dynamically compressed materials.read more →

    We explore unusual chemical bonding and mechanochemistry at extreme conditions to predict and synthesize novel high energy density materials. read more →

    Discovery Science with super computers and machine learning

    We devise machine-learning models of interatomic interactions and utilize state-of-the-art pre-exascale GPU supercomputers to achieve quantum accuracy in predictive MD simulations of materials at experimentally relevant time and length scales. read more →
